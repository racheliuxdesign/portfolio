Threat Dashboard Redesign

Redesigned the central threat monitoring dashboard to surface critical insights faster and reduce analyst response time.

Role Senior Product Designer
Duration 4 months
Team Designer, 3 Engineers, PM, Data Analyst
Hero screenshot placeholder

Overview

The threat monitoring dashboard was the primary interface for security analysts tracking real-time incidents and vulnerabilities. As the product matured and data volume grew, the original layout could no longer effectively communicate critical information at a glance. A full redesign was needed to match the evolving needs of security operations teams.

Problem

Analysts were overwhelmed by dense, undifferentiated data panels. Key metrics were buried among less important information, and the lack of visual hierarchy made it difficult to quickly assess the current threat landscape. Response times suffered as analysts had to navigate through multiple views to piece together the full picture.

Before state — original dashboard layout

Design Process

Spent time shadowing security analysts during their workflows to understand which information they prioritized and how they triaged alerts. Used card sorting and co-design sessions to establish an information hierarchy. Developed a modular widget system that let analysts customize their view while ensuring critical alerts were always prominent. Iterated through high-fidelity prototypes with real data to validate readability and scanning patterns.

Design exploration — layout and widget concepts
Final design — redesigned threat dashboard

Outcome & Impact

The redesigned dashboard reduced average threat identification time by roughly 35%. Analysts reported significantly improved situational awareness and the customizable widget system became one of the product's key differentiators in competitive evaluations.
