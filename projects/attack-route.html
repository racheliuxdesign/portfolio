Attack Route Visualization

Value at a Glance

🔍

Identify and analyze potential threats to your organization

⚡

Assess the likelihood of exploitation of your cyber vulnerabilities

🗺️

Obtain a comprehensive, mapped view at the company level of all types of risks, including internet-facing assets, cloud services, on-premises access, and vendors.

Personas

  • CISO
  • C-level management
  • Organizational decision-makers
🌫️

Challenge — Show only the important stuff

Each vulnerability or asset detected can have a lot of properties attached to it in the attack graph. All of that can create noise that distracts users from the issues that need to be addressed NOW.

💡

The Solution

Implement a system of highlights and filters, some ready-made and some triggered by user selection. As a result, users can focus on what matters most to them.

🧩

Challenge: I understand the problem, what do I do next?

Once users identifies the vulnerabilities they want to address in their organization based on the attack graph data, they should initiate the mitigation process. Our goal is to guide them in selecting and incorporating these vulnerabilities into a work plan.

💡

The Solution

Using the plan calculator, users can select and remove vulnerabilities from a simulation plan. They can also test how the plan will impact their department's budget, the predicted improvements in the attack graph, and the amount of time required for implementation.

If the user is satisfied with the simulation, they can save the plan and sync it with their ticketing system (e.g., Jira). This allows them to track the progress of mitigation and its impact on the organization's risk and maturity.

🔌

Challenge: There are vulnerabilities in my business that your system has failed to find. Can I centralize everything in one platform?

Despite having many integrations, our platform cannot discover all data. Customers typically use 20 different security tools simultaneously. To correctly prioritize all vulnerabilities, they need to be consolidated in one place. Therefore, we need to enable users to add vulnerabilities and assets to the system and prioritize them, providing stakeholders with an accurate picture of the situation.
